Wall Cabinet

50″ wide max, sits above the deck box. Inverter left, Tycon right.

Size: 49.5″ W × 29.5″ H × 11″ D

Interior 48 × 28 × 10¼. Fits your 50″ limit. Height is set by the Tycon, which mounts vertically and needs 7.9″ of open air above and below for convection, so 28″ interior is the floor in any orientation.

Why three thermal zones

Zone A · inverter (left)

Your biggest heat source, and it sits directly above the deck-box gland plate so the stiff 2/0 rises straight up with the shortest possible run. Own intake below, exhaust + thermostatic fan above. Fans kick on at 120°F, case hits 140°F.

Zone B · Iota + ATS (middle)

Stacked to save width. The Iota still makes ~90W of heat when charging and its label forbids a zero-clearance mount, so it gets real air space. The ATS is moisture-sensitive, hence up off the bottom.

Zone C · Tycon (right)

Passively cooled, no fan, so it needs its own uninterrupted convection column: intake low, exhaust high, nothing stacked above or below it. Separating it from the inverter means they stop cooking each other.

Non-negotiables

Nothing in this box is weather-rated. The Tycon is IP43, the ATS is indoor-only, the inverter and Iota are indoor. The cabinet is the weather barrier: sloped cap, sealed seams, bottom-only entries with drip loops.

Two devices demand a non-combustible mount. The inverter manual says so outright, and the Iota label says do not install near flammable materials. Plywood is flammable. Standoffs solve the barrier and the air gap together.

Iota: insert the dual-voltage plug. Default 27.2V is a float voltage and will not properly charge LiFePO4. The plug switches it to 28.4V, which will.
