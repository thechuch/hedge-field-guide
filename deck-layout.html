Deck Box Layout

Top-down, drawn to scale so you can plan positions and estimate cut lengths.

Fixed vs proposed (read first)

Fixed (measured): box 53.3 x 26.5 x 24 in, six 8.2 x 21 in batteries in a row, terminals at the FRONT (away from the house), the 16 x 48 in board on 2 in standoffs above.

Proposed (you set these): the bus bars are drawn SPREAD, one per battery pair, so the battery cables stay short (the trade is longer bar-to-bar jumpers). Cluster them in the middle instead and the outer battery cables get much longer.

Confirm on your parts: the Joinfworld 4-stud bars are likely 3/8 in (M10) studs, not M8. Measure one battery post-to-post spacing (assumed 4 in) and which post is +.

"Cut to" = straight run + 2 in rise + lugs + 15% slack, rounded up. Internal 2/0 for this layout ≈ 26.3 ft. Cabinet feeds add their rise to the wall cabinet (measure separately).

How to actually cut 2/0 (do not trust a drawing for the final cut)

Stiff 2/0 will not follow straight lines. Use these numbers to ORDER cable and plan, then cut each run to fit:

  1. Mount the bars and fuse holders first, in these positions. Nothing moves after this.
  2. Run a string (or a scrap cable) from each terminal to its stud along the REAL path, including the rise to the board and a gentle bend radius (no kinks).
  3. Add ~2 in of slack (a small service loop) so a re-torque or re-lug will not leave it short. Mark, then cut.
  4. Crimp, heat-shrink, dry-fit before the next one. Cut the longest/outer runs first so offcuts can become shorter inner runs.
