Heated Chicken Waterer

DIY insulated reservoir + nipples, run straight off the 24V bank.

The build (recommended)

One insulated 5–7 gal reservoir bucket with the heater inside, feeding a short drinker bar of ~6 horizontal poultry nipples (about 1 per 3 birds, no crowding). Not an open stock tank (fouls, freezes fastest, wastes the most battery). Not two heaters — one reservoir, optionally two nipple bars off it.

Keep the 600W element you found. Energy used = heat lost, not element size, so a big element on a thermostat costs the same watt-hours as a small one; it just cycles less. Insulate hard and wire it for its 25A.

Wiring (the 25A path)

Bank 24V+ → 30A fuse → junction C → [snap-disc over-temp N.C.] → contactor → ELEMENT(+) ELEMENT(−) → 24V− Contactor COIL ← thermostat / ESP32 (low current) + flyback diode across the coil

Rides the shared water trench as circuit E5 (24V DC is trench-safe with the pipe). Fuse it at the solar box.

Control & safety

Primary control = standalone DC thermostat

Not the ESP32 — chickens need water even if the network is down. The thermostat just switches the contactor coil.

Let the ESP32 watch it

A waterproof DS18B20 + the float switch feed the dashboard for a low-water alert and a dry-run alarm, without the water depending on it.

Hardware failsafe

The load-rated snap-disc opens at ~130°F if the contactor welds or the element dry-fires. Belt and suspenders.

Never heat dry

The float keeps it topped; the float switch tells the ESP32 to inhibit the heater and refill if the level drops.

Energy reality — budget for it

A well-insulated bucket in the teens averages roughly 1–2 kWh/day; a poorly insulated one or a hard multi-day freeze can hit ~3 kWh/day. That stacks on the freezer at the worst solar time. Insulation is what keeps you at 1 instead of 3.
