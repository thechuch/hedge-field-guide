Shopping List

What to grab locally vs what to order. Green bar = grab it this trip.

Do NOT buy yet

No ring lugs. No charger fuses. Both depend on measuring your bus stud size (M8 vs 3/8″ M10) and confirming your Iota model. Buying blind means wrong terminals. They are Amazon items anyway.

🌾 Grange Co-op

  • Horizontal poultry nipples ×8Need 6 for 20 birds, spares are pennies. Heart of the waterer build.
  • Float valve (stock-tank or poultry)Auto-fill. Keeps the heating element submerged. Run-dry kills the element.
  • Food-grade bucket, 5 to 7 galThe waterer reservoir.
  • Drip filters, 120 to 200 mesh ×2Protects emitters from pond water. Open item.
  • Low-pressure drip tape4 to 10 PSI type, matches the ~10 PSI submersible. Zucchini + trees.
  • 1″ poly pipe + barb fittings, tees, stainless clampsZone lines and the mini-barrel run.
  • 1″ barb × 1″ MNPT adaptersNeeded to put the two Eastman check valves inline. Long-standing blocker.
  • Landscape fabricGoes in the tank pad box BEFORE the rock.
  • Teflon tapeYou will use a whole roll.
  • Chest cooler (if they have one)Doubles as the insulated battery box for the shed 2P bank.

🔧 Hardware store · Round 2

  • Digital caliper (~$12)Settles the bus stud size (M8 vs 3/8″ M10). Unblocks the 2/0 + lug order. Measure a stud in the parking lot.
  • Heat gun (~$25)The adhesive heat shrink you bought needs it. A lighter scorches it and ruins the seal.
  • 1-5/8″ hole sawFor the ~1.65″ bulkhead hole in the waterer bucket.
  • Drill bit for the nipples + 1/8″ NPT tap if threadedREAD the nipple package for the required hole size before buying the bit.
  • Food-safe siliconeInsurance on the bulkhead gasket and nipple threads.
  • 1/4″ polycarbonate (Lexan) sheet + nylon standoffs + nylon thumbscrewsThe lift-off bus guard. All non-conductive on purpose.
  • Split wire loomSleeve the unfused battery-to-fuse stubs and dress cable.
  • Black heat shrinkYour roll is red; you want black for the negatives.
  • Wire labels or a paint penLabel studs (P-A1 etc.) and cables per the stud map.
  • DIN rail (short length)Only if you lack one for the TPDIN + ATO fuse block on the left wall.

🔧 Hardware store · Round 1

✅ Already bought: anti-seize, torque wrench, cable clamps. Still open below.

  • Cement board or aluminum sheetThe non-combustible barrier under the bus bars + fuses. They are on bare plywood right now.
  • 1/2″ FxF brass couplings ×4Mandatory at the SEAFLO's male ports. All your couplings are 3/4″.
  • 1/2″ PVC pipe + tee + end capThe drinker bar for the nipples.
  • Rigid foam boardBucket insulation. The #1 energy lever on the waterer.
  • UV-black zip ties
  • Rect-to-round downspout adapterOnly if you are touching the rainwater line this trip.

📦 Amazon (do not hunt locally)

2/0 cable + lugs, hydraulic crimper, adhesive heat shrink, terminal boots, 70A/30A charger fuses + holders, 6ga/10ga lugs, DC clamp meter (UNI-T UT210E), 40A DC contactor, snap-disc over-temp, DC thermostat, float switch.

Shed system: 2 × ~400W panels, ~40A MPPT, 300 to 600W low-idle pure sine inverter, solar exhaust fan.

If you only grab three

Poultry nipples, the float valve, and the 1″ barb × 1″ MNPT adapters. The first two unblock the waterer build; the third has been blocking your check valves for weeks.
