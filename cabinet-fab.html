Cabinet Fab Sheet

Welded aluminum electrical enclosure. Outdoor, wall-mounted.

Material

1/8″ (0.125″) 5052-H32 aluminum sheet. 5052 rather than 6061: better corrosion resistance and it forms and welds cleanly for enclosure work. Drop to 0.090″ if you prefer, saves roughly 15 lb.

All seams welded watertight. This lives outside on a wall with no roof overhang, and nothing inside it is weather-rated.

Method is your call. If you would rather brake-form a wrapper than weld five flat panels, go for it. The dimensions below are what matter.

Scope

🔧 Weld shop

1. Box shell: back, 2 sides, bottom, sloped top.
2. All seams watertight.
3. 1″ inward flange around the front opening (the face the door gasket seals against).
4. 2 doors, flat, cut to fit the opening.
5. 4 studs (1/4-20) welded inside the back + one flat inner panel drilled to match.
6. 1 grounding stud welded inside.
7. Mounting flanges as part of the back panel.

Please leave it otherwise undrilled. No vent holes, no gland holes, no hinge or latch holes. I will do all of those.

Why the details matter (context, not instructions)

The sloped top with the front overhang is the whole weather strategy. There is no roof over this wall, so water has to run off the front and drip clear of the doors.

The removable inner panel lets me mount and wire ~50 lb of gear at a bench and then drop the assembly in, instead of working overhead inside a box on a wall.

Undrilled mounting flanges because I have to find the framing in the wall first and drill to match.

Aluminum, not steel: no rust on an exposed wall, and it is ~110 lb lighter, which matters because the house is on a trailer.

Interior it yields

Roughly 49.75 × 29.75 × 10.75. The gear needs 45.7″ of width and the charge controller needs 7.9″ of open air above and below it, so there is comfortable margin on both.
